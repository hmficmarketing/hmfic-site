      - "Mission First Pickleball is a first responder owned paddle and apparel brand. The founders actually play the game, and they built the company around a simple idea. Serve communities with their day jobs. Serve with purpose on the court."
      - "They came to us the way good clients usually do. One of the partners was already a long time client on another brand, so when they launched MFP with their team, they wanted the same agency running marketing and ads. The trust was already there."
      - "The brief itself was straightforward. Launch phase. New paddle line starting at $115. A second pro level paddle dropping in April at $150, a third later at $199. Revenue goal starts modest and scales with inventory. Target audience is the pickleball player who is done dabbling. They want a brand to believe in, not another hype paddle."
      - "There was one problem. The site they wanted us to send ad traffic to was not going to convert."

  - type: text
    chapterNumber: "03"
    chapterName: "The Strategy"
    title: "Fix the store before spending a dollar on traffic."
    paragraphs:
      - "Before we spent a dollar on ads we audited the store the founders had stood up. The post click flow was broken. Products were hard to browse. There were no real product pages, no clear buy buttons, no about section telling the brand story. Nothing on that site was doing the job ads were about to send people to do."
      - "We made the call on the phone. We could run ads, but with a brand new brand plus a weak site, that is a losing battle on conversions. Every dollar of ad spend would be fighting two fights at once. The smarter move was to rebuild the store from scratch, then turn traffic on."
      - "Thirty minutes later we sent back a mockup. Brand story front and center. Colors dialed in. Visually different from every other pickleball paddle brand on the market. The mockup ended the conversation. No pushback. No debate on timeline. We were off to the races."
    callout:
      label: "Decision"
      body: "Rebuild the store before running ads. Ad budget is only as good as the site it lands on."

    title: "Every page, every decision, in order."
    paragraphs:
      - "The rebuild was a full Shopify store. Homepage, product page, collection page, about, contact, ad landing page, blog. Eight pages, wired together on a theme we customized heavily so MFP did not look like another Dawn clone."
      - "We did not stop at the storefront. We built out the post visit experience too. A Klaviyo popup opt in on the homepage captures email before first time visitors leave. A PostScript SMS sequence is wired into the same flow so subscribers land in both channels without stepping on each other. Every new lead goes into a welcome sequence that keeps them engaged after the first click, before the first purchase, and through every launch after that."
      - "There was no dramatic crisis during the build. Every page had its own tuning. Fonts, layout, image crops, how the copy landed in context. It is easy to make something good. We fine tuned it to make it great. Then we pushed traffic and kept iterating."
