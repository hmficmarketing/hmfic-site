# HMFIC Site Copy Rewrite — Design Spec

> Date: 2026-03-20
> Status: Draft
> Scope: Copy and content rewrite of the single-page HMFIC Marketing site (`index.html`)

## Problem

The current site positions HMFIC as a generic media buyer ("The #1 Ad System for Scaling Your Brand to 7 Figures"). It makes no mention of the Gravity Converter Formula (the proprietary client intelligence framework), the AI-powered content engine, or the Hybrid Agency model. The copy could belong to any ads agency. It doesn't differentiate.

## Goal

Reposition the site copy to reflect the real business: ads-first, system-powered, AI-accelerated. The Gravity Converter Formula is the differentiator. AI is the accelerant, not the product. Content and ads are part of the same system, not separate services.

**Approach:** Ads-First, System-Powered (Option B from brainstorming). Lead with the core promise (we run your ads and scale your brand), then reveal why it works differently — the GCF, the intelligence layer, the AI execution.

## Constraints

- Structure stays the same: Hero, About, Services, Logos, Testimonials, Offer, Why Us, Apply, Footer
- No specific packages or pricing (tiers are not defined yet)
- CTA remains "Apply to Work With Us"
- GCF is named but phases are NOT revealed by name and the 55-question count is NOT mentioned
- AI is positioned as a superpower built on the system — not a buzzword, not a shortcut
- SMS Marketing is folded into the broader system, no longer a standalone service card
- No dashes in written copy (per Matt's directive — use arrows, periods, or rewrite)

## Section-by-Section Spec

### 1. Hero

**Change level:** Major rewrite

**Badge:** Keep "Facebook · Google · TikTok Ads" — grounds the page immediately.

**Headline:** Two parts. First anchors on ads/scaling. Second introduces the Gravity Converter Formula by name. Territory: "We Scale Brands With Paid Ads — Powered by the Gravity Converter Formula." The GCF name creates curiosity and signals a proprietary system.

**Subhead:** Unpacks the GCF at a high level. It's a client intelligence framework that maps your audience, your messaging, and your content strategy before a dollar is spent. AI accelerates the execution, but the system is what makes it hit. Your ads, content, and scripts speak to your audience where they are and move them from attention to action.

**CTAs:** Keep both.
- Primary: "Apply to Work With Us"
- Secondary: "See How It Works" (scrolls to Services/system section)

**Proof bar:** 3 items. Keep "Proven 3-5X ROI." Replace "Hands-Free Management" and "Limited Spots Available" — one should communicate the AI-as-superpower philosophy (territory: "Built for Where Marketing Is Going" or "AI as a Superpower, Not a Shortcut"), one should keep the scarcity/exclusivity angle.

### 2. About Matt

**Change level:** Moderate rewrite

**Core narrative shift:** From resume to founder story. Three beats:

1. **Origin:** You started running ads because business owners needed someone working IN their ads manager so they could work ON their business. That hasn't changed.
2. **Evolution:** But the game expanded. AI is moving fast. Content marketing is exploding. The brands with money know they need to leverage all of it — they just don't have time to figure it out and do it right. That's where you come in.
3. **The system:** You built the Gravity Converter Formula because you were tired of running campaigns on surface-level info. You wanted a real intelligence system that maps who the audience is, what they respond to, and why — before you ever build an ad. And now AI accelerates the whole thing, powered by that intelligence, not by templates.

**Key lines to preserve or adapt:**
- "HMFIC isn't an agency with junior account managers" — keep
- The "not handed off" differentiator — keep

**Stats:** Keep "$10M+ Ad Spend Managed" and "3-5X Average Client ROI." Consider adding a third if one emerges (clients served, content pieces generated, etc.) but don't force it.

### 3. Services (The Three Engines)

**Change level:** Major rewrite — new cards replacing the current 3

**Card 1: Intelligence**
The GCF at work. Before any ad runs or content is created, we map your business, your audience, and your messaging. We figure out what your people respond to, what's holding them back, what moves them to act — at every level. This isn't a questionnaire. It's a client intelligence framework that everything else is built on.

**Card 2: Authority (Content Engine)**
The intelligence feeds a content system — ads, scripts, social content, email — all built to speak to your audience where they are. Not generic templates. Content that sounds like you because it's grounded in real data about your business and your customers. AI accelerates the output, but the system is why it hits.

**Card 3: Acceleration (Paid Ads Engine)**
The core — Facebook, Google, TikTok ads built on real intelligence, not guesswork. Every campaign is informed by the same system that drives the content. Ads and content are the same conversation. That's how you compound results instead of just spending money.

**Note:** The ordering (Intelligence → Authority → Acceleration) tells a story of how the system works, but copy within each card must NOT imply "content first, ads second." Card 3 makes clear ads are the engine. Content amplifies.

**SMS:** Folded into Cards 2 and 3 as a tactic within the system. No standalone card.

## Messaging Principles (For Copy Writing)

1. **Ads are the anchor.** Never let the page accidentally read as "content agency." Ads are the core. Content multiplies.
2. **Name the GCF.** Use the name. It builds brand equity and curiosity. But don't reveal the internal structure (phases, sections, question count).
3. **AI is a superpower, not a product.** Position as: we've adapted, we're ahead, the system makes AI work the right way. Never "AI-powered" as a buzzword.
4. **Speak to the busy owner.** Your ideal client sees the opportunity in AI and content but doesn't have time to do it all. You're the person who already built the system and stays ahead so they don't have to.
5. **Outcomes over features.** Frame deliverables as what the system produces, not a menu of services.
6. **No dashes.** Arrows, periods, or rewrite the sentence.
